
🔍 LLM Hallucination Detector

"ChatGPT said it. But is it actually true?"

A real-time hallucination detection system that sits inside your browser, automatically checks AI responses against retrieved evidence, and tells you whether to trust the answer — before you act on it.

🚨 The Problem
Every day, millions of people trust AI answers that are completely wrong.

A US lawyer submitted fake court cases generated by ChatGPT — and got fined by a judge
Students submit invented research citations that don't exist
Professionals make decisions based on fabricated statistics

There is no warning. No flag. No way to know. Until now.

✅ What This Does
You ask ChatGPT a question
         ↓
ChatGPT gives an answer
         ↓
Our system fetches real evidence from trusted sources
         ↓
Fine-tuned DeBERTa-v3 checks consistency
         ↓
Chrome Extension shows ✅ Factual or ⚠️ Hallucination
— right inside the ChatGPT page
No copy-pasting. No separate tools. Just an instant badge on every response.

🧠 How It Works
1. RAG Pipeline
When an AI response is detected, the system embeds the question using sentence-transformers and searches a FAISS vector index for the most relevant real-world evidence.
2. DeBERTa-v3 Classifier
A fine-tuned DeBERTa-v3-base model (184M parameters) takes the AI answer and retrieved evidence as input and outputs:

✅ FACTUAL — answer is consistent with evidence
⚠️ HALLUCINATION — answer contradicts real sources
❓ UNCERTAIN — not enough evidence to judge

3. Chrome Extension
Results are injected directly into the ChatGPT/Gemini interface as a badge — no extra steps needed.
